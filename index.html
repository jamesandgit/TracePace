How is this calculated?
  • Everything comes from one simulation of the loan — the hero figure, the chart, and the schedule always agree.
  • Interest accrues daily (Actual/365 — odd month lengths and leap years included) and is charged monthly, the way Australian lenders do it.
  • Because the standard repayment formula assumes equal months, the loan can finish a month or so either side of the nominal term — real loans do the same.
  • New loan: the contractual repayment is the standard amortising amount P = L·r / (1 − (1+r)⁻ⁿ) at your chosen frequency. Existing loan: your entered repayment is used as-is.
  • Weekly / fortnightly figures are true amortising payments (≈ monthly × 12 ÷ 52 or ÷ 26) — not the lender "divide by 4" shortcut.
  • Offset reduces the balance that interest is charged on. Extra repayments and rate changes are dated events; each rate change re-amortises the contractual payment over the remaining term.
  • During an interest-only period, payments cover interest and the balance holds still.
  • The rate comparison excludes rate-change events so it stays a pure rate-vs-rate test.

How is this calculated?
  • After-tax income uses ATO 2024–25 resident brackets plus the 2% Medicare levy, with the low-income shade-in and family thresholds for couples/dependants.
  • Living expenses are floored at a simplified HEM benchmark (≈ $1,750/mo single, ≈ $3,100/mo couple, + $550 per dependant, scaling with income) — lenders assess max(declared, HEM).
  • 85% of your monthly surplus is treated as available for repayments.
  • The maximum loan is what that repayment services at the APRA-style assessment rate.
